Successful Missions

  • Apollo 11 – Saturn V

    • Apollo 11 was a landmark success in human history, achieving the first crewed lunar landing on July 20, 1969, and returning astronauts safely to Earth on July 24. Commander Neil Armstrong and Lunar Module Pilot Buzz Aldrin landed the Eagle in the Sea of Tranquility,fulfilling the U.S. goal to reach the Moon.

  • Mars Orbiter Mission – PSLV

    • The Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV), known as ISRO's "workhorse", is one of the world’s most reliable rockets, boasting a ~95% success rate over 60+ launches since 1993. It is famous for launching over 350 satellites, including historic missions like Chandrayaan-1, Mars Orbiter (Mangalyaan), and a record-setting 104 satellites in one flight.

  • Chandrayaan-3 – LVM3

    • ISRO's Chandrayaan-3 mission was completely successful, making India the first nation to land near the lunar South Pole and the fourth overall to achieve a soft landing on the Moon. The Vikram lander touched down on August 23, 2023, initiating a 14-day mission involving the Pragyan rover.

  • Falcon Heavy Test Flight

    • SpaceX's Falcon Heavy test flight on February 6, 2018, was a major success, demonstrating the rocket's capability to carry heavy payloads to orbit. The launch featured a unique payload—a Tesla Roadster—and successfully landed two of the three boosters back on Earth, showcasing SpaceX's advancements in reusable rocket technology.

Failed Missions

  • Space Shuttle Challenger (1986)

    • The 1986 Space Shuttle Challenger disaster was caused by the failure of O-ring seals in the right solid rocket booster (SRB) to seal in cold temperatures (38°F/3°C) allowing hot gas to breach the joint 73 seconds after liftoff. This failure was exacerbated by a flawed design, poor management decisions, and ignored warnings

  • Space Shuttle Columbia (2003)

    • The Space Shuttle Columbia disaster occurred on February 1, 2003, when the shuttle disintegrated during re-entry into Earth's atmosphere. The cause was damage to the left wing's thermal protection system, which was struck by a piece of foam insulation that broke off from the external fuel tank during launch. This damage allowed hot gases to penetrate the wing structure, leading to the shuttle's breakup and the loss of all seven crew members.

  • Early Falcon 1 launches

    • SpaceX's Falcon 1 experienced three consecutive launch failures between 2006 and 2008. The first failure in March 2006 was due to a fuel leak caused by a corroded aluminum nut. The second failure in March 2007 resulted from a roll control issue caused by a fuel line rupture. The third failure in August 2008 was due to a stage separation problem, where the first and second stages failed to separate properly, leading to the rocket's destruction.
