Kennedy Space Center

Kennedy Space Center (KSC) Launch Complex 39, located on Florida's Merritt Island, is a historic, primary NASA site for human spaceflight, featuring pads 39A and 39B built in the 1960s for Apollo/Saturn V. It later hosted 53 Space Shuttle missions. Currently, Pad 39A is leased by SpaceX for Falcon 9/Heavy and Starship, while 39B supports NASA’s Artemis SLS rocket.

Satish Dhawan Space Centre

The Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC) SHAR, located in Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh, is India's premier spaceport, operated by ISRO. It currently operates two active launch pads for PSLV, GSLV, and LVM3 rockets, with a third launch pad approved in January 2025 to support future Next Generation Launch Vehicles (NGLV).

Starbase Texas

SpaceX Starbase in South Texas is the primary development, manufacturing, and launch site for the Starship rocket, featuring an active orbital launch pad (OLP-1) with a massive "Mechazilla" tower for catching boosters. As of early 2026, the facility is rapidly expanding, with a second launch tower (OLT-3) under construction to increase launch cadence for the world's most powerful rocket.

Baikonur Cosmodrome

The Baikonur Cosmodrome, located in Kazakhstan, is the world's first and largest operational space launch facility. It has been the primary launch site for Soviet and Russian space missions since the 1950s, including the first human spaceflight by Yuri Gagarin in 1961.

Cape Canaveral

Cape Canaveral Space Force Station, located in Florida, is a major American space launch facility. It has been the primary launch site for many historic space missions, including the Apollo program and the Space Shuttle program.

Guiana Space Center

The Guiana Space Center, located in French Guiana, is Europe's only active spaceport. It is operated by Arianespace and serves as the launch site for Ariane rockets, including the Ariane 5 and Ariane 6.

Wenchang Spacecraft Launch Site

The Wenchang Spacecraft Launch Site, located in Hainan Province, China, is a modern space launch facility. It is primarily used for launching the Long March series of rockets and has been the site of many important space missions.
